Our Foundation

Experience That Spans the Entire Ecosystem

The Compeer management team is composed of senior healthcare subject matter experts. Our team represents a cross-section of industry experience with a deep history around information exchange and business process improvement. Our health information technology expertise is blended with a unique understanding of clinical data, Medicare, and Medicaid — giving us powerful business insights that generalist consultants simply can't match.

Chris Smith

Founder & CEO

Chris Smith is a Subject Matter Expert in Health Information Technology architecture, systems, and infrastructure, with a specialization in clinical data technical strategy. A well-known and published author, speaker, and industry committee member, Chris has spent his career shaping how clinical data flows across the healthcare landscape.

Among his career highlights, Chris was part of one of the first teams to implement internet connectivity to the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) — a foundational milestone in healthcare data exchange. His breadth of experience spans Health Information Exchange design, standards development, and clinical data architecture at national and international scales. His hands-on work includes Medicaid IT modernization, FHIR API compliance with the CMS Final Rule, and bi-directional EHR clinical data exchange for state Medicaid agencies.

BS Healthcare Administration — College of Saint Scholastica MBA (IT Focus) — Augsburg University HIE Architecture FHIR & CMS Compliance Medicaid IT Modernization

Kevin Erickson

Founder & CTO

Kevin Erickson has an in-depth understanding of operations and IT management in the healthcare payer industry. With more than 25 years of experience across healthcare, information technology, operations management, business process improvement, and business development, Kevin brings a breadth of knowledge that bridges the business and technical worlds.

Kevin spent over two decades at Noridian, a Blue Cross Blue Shield plan and Medicare Administrative Contractor, developing deep expertise in Medicare payer operations from the ground up. That foundation — spanning technical systems, business operations, and regulatory compliance — informs his approach to every engagement. He has worked closely with stakeholders in the provider, payer, and regulatory fields across Medicare, Medicaid, and commercial payer environments.

BS Computer Science — North Dakota State University Medicare Administrative Contractor (MAC) Expertise Healthcare Payer Operations Medicare & Medicaid Business Process Improvement

Ian Morris

COO

Ian Morris has more than 15 years of experience in healthcare interoperability, with a focus on connecting providers to Medicare, Medicaid, VA, and Social Security Administration systems. He has successfully managed projects for payers and providers, exchanging both medical records and claims data across complex institutional boundaries.

In 2016, Ian completed a first-of-its-kind project connecting a large academic medical center to a Medicaid agency for the exchange of patient clinical summaries — a milestone that demonstrated the real-world viability of large-scale, cross-sector clinical data sharing. His experience includes clinical data interoperability program management for state Medicaid agencies, including data lake architecture and governance initiatives. Prior to Compeer, Ian held roles at UnitedHealthcare and McKesson, and has worked as an independent consultant across the healthcare IT space.

BA — University of Minnesota Healthcare Interoperability Clinical Data Exchange Medicaid Systems Data Governance

Richard Zhang

Chief Architect

Richard Zhang is an expert in IT service delivery with a specialization in healthcare interoperability and analytics. His career includes successful implementations of large-scale healthcare IT projects, including an innovative, high-speed Enterprise Master Patient Index (EMPI) — a technically demanding system at the heart of patient-matching and data integrity.

Richard brings enterprise pedigree from his time at Deloitte Consulting, SAP, and Sun Microsystems — where he was named Employee of the Year. His combination of global enterprise consulting experience with deep healthcare domain knowledge makes him uniquely suited to architect complex, scalable interoperability solutions. He continues to publish actively on emerging topics including generative AI implementation and healthcare data governance.

BS Electronic Physics — Peking University MS Engineering Sciences — Dartmouth Cert. Technology & Value Chain — MIT Sloan Healthcare Analytics AI & Data Governance
